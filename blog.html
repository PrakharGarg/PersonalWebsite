Week 1

(Jan/16 - Jan/22)

  • What did you do this past week?

    This past week I moved back to Austin from my home in Dallas and prepared for my final semester of college. After attending both lectures of Software Engineering (SWE), I started to freshen up on Python and JavaScript.

  • What's in your way?

    The biggest thing currently in my way is that I do not know how to prepare for Software Engineering. Other than reading the material listed on Professor Downing's website, I am not sure what else to do.

  • What will you do next week?

    Next week I plan on beginning the first project and refreshing my knowledge of Python. I also plan on actually preparing for the quizzes, instead of simply skimming over the material like I did for the first quiz.

  • What's my experience of the class?

    My initial experience of the class is that I am wary of letting the work pile up. Having taken another project-intensive course (iOS Mobile Computing) I know that if I do not keep up with the work, this class can very easily cause a lot of stress.

  • What's my pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

    My pick of the week would be: HackDesign's website. If you are like me and are terrible at front-end development, sign-up for their mailing-list and they will send you 1 lesson a week to complete.

    I would also recommend looking at Virtualenv for local Python development.

Week 3

(Jan/30 - Feb/5)

  • What did you do this past week?

    This past week I finished the Collatz project early in the week, so I would have plenty of time later to make sure I completed all of the steps in the workflow. Finishing the project early, also made my week pretty laid back. I also started preparing a lot more for the daily quizzes and have been doing a lot better on them.

  • What's in your way?

    The biggest thing currently is trying to keep focused on school. This is my last semester and I have already signed on with a company for full-time, thus I am eager to graduate. Also, I need to find a better way to ingest all of the readings and actually understand and remember all of the material, instead of simply skimming over it and forgetting the majority of the content.

  • What will you do next week?

    Next week I plan on beginning the second project. I had a lot of success by starting early on the first project, and hopefully I can do the same for this upcoming one. I also want to get more familiar with Python and flask, because I heard that later on projects rely heavily on flask.

  • What's my experience of the class?

    My experience of the class so far is that I really enjoy the style of Professor Downing's class. I like that the class is not just a lecture, but more of a back and forth with the students. I feel more engaged in this class than my other classes, and also feel like I retain the information covered in class a lot better.

  • What's my pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

    My pick of the week is a list of extremely useful atom packages. Week 9

(Mar/20 - Mar/26)

  • What did you do this past week?

    Coming back from spring break was rough. As a senior eager to graduate, spring break really made me lose focus and what little motivation I had left. Despite some major senioritis, however, my group and I successfully completed Phase 1 of the IDB project. While we ran into a small hiccup with one of the project requirements being unclear, I am extremely proud of all of the work that everyone put in. Other than working on the project, I had a pretty light week in terms of school work.

  • What's in your way?

    With only a month left of school, finding a reason to really care about my classes has been hard. While I haven't hit the point of outright forgoing my two classes, I am extremely content with simply riding out the last month with minimum effort. After today (Sunday), we only have 6 weeks left of class. I also have no finals, so May 6th is a day that I am really looking forward to.

  • What will you do next week?

    Next week I plan on beginning Phase 2 of the project. I was a little uncomfortable with how last minute we completed Phase 1, so I would definitely like to start early this time. My other class, Data Management, is also starting a final project so I will have to start working on that as well.

  • What's my experience of the class?

    I was really disappointed by the project specs given to us for Phase 1. After being forced to read so many articles on Agile, Extreme Programming, and general best practices for managing development, receiving a pretty vague and not updated requirement doc for Phase 1 was frustrating. There were a lot of Piazza posts that had to be made that could have easily been resolved with a clearer spec sheet. Also, there were many components of this phase that seemed like a waste of time and could have easily been moved to the next phase where it would be more practical. Both Model.py and Tests.py did not really need to be in this phase: both could not be run/tested so we had no way to know if what we did was right.

  • What's my pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

    My pick of the week is a the Hackathon Hackers fb group It's a community of people around the world who are interested in hackathon, building cool things, and tech in general. I always see really active discussions about important topics, and the community also has a lot of resources if you are trying to learn something new.
