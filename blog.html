Week 3

(Jan/30 - Feb/5)

  • What did you do this past week?

    This past week I finished the Collatz project early in the week, so I would have plenty of time later to make sure I completed all of the steps in the workflow. Finishing the project early, also made my week pretty laid back. I also started preparing a lot more for the daily quizzes and have been doing a lot better on them.

  • What's in your way?

    The biggest thing currently is trying to keep focused on school. This is my last semester and I have already signed on with a company for full-time, thus I am eager to graduate. Also, I need to find a better way to ingest all of the readings and actually understand and remember all of the material, instead of simply skimming over it and forgetting the majority of the content.

  • What will you do next week?

    Next week I plan on beginning the second project. I had a lot of success by starting early on the first project, and hopefully I can do the same for this upcoming one. I also want to get more familiar with Python and flask, because I heard that later on projects rely heavily on flask.

  • What's my experience of the class?

    My experience of the class so far is that I really enjoy the style of Professor Downing's class. I like that the class is not just a lecture, but more of a back and forth with the students. I feel more engaged in this class than my other classes, and also feel like I retain the information covered in class a lot better.

  • What's my pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

    My pick of the week is a list of extremely useful atom packages. I know a lot of people don't like atom because it is a web browser and can get slow at times, but I personally feel that atom has a lot stronger open source community behind it than sublime. Also, since the text-editor is a web browser, it has a lot of support for web development.

  • What did you do this past week?

    This past week I started the Netflix project and spent a lot of time reading up on the Netflix problem. After the collatz project, I feel a lot more comfortable with the workflow of the projects, and setting up the project took considerably less time this time around.

  • What's in your way?

    Next week I plan on finishing the Netflix project. So far, I have finished Netflix.py and have gotten my RMS down to .97. All I need to do now is to figure out how to test my program and write the unit tests.

  • What's my experience of the class?

    My experience of the class so far is that the class is a lot "easier" than I imagined. In all honesty, the workflow outlined makes up for 80% of the work and the actual project is actually quite trivial.

  • What's my pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

    My pick of the week is the objectified documentary. If you are interested in design, Objectified is a really good documentary on all things design. The documentary really makes you look a common day items that we take for granted differently, and think about how each of them were designed to fulfill a specific function. Apple's Jon Ive even makes an appearance and talks about the design of their iPod and MacBooks.
